AABC 2015 Jog-a-Thon!

Dear AABC Parents: On the afternoon of Saturday, April 18th, we’ll conduct our 33rd annual Jog-A-Thon at the Davis Middle School Track (directly across from the fairgrounds on Arlington Street). Tuition covers 20% of our annual budget. Concerts and fundraisers—like the Jog-a-Thon, Holiday appeal, fall gala Christmas and summer concerts—make up the difference; and their success enables us to keep tuition as low as possible. The Jog-A-Thon is the largest fund-raiser for the Chorus. It isn’t a fund-raiser for “frills” or “extras”—rather the income is needed to pay our ongoing, everyday expenses. Concert and Touring Chorus members (“red shirts”) and Training Division members (“blue shirts”) must participate in this extremely crucial fund-raising effort. Audition Division members (“white shirts”) are not required to participate, but are highly encouraged to do so. Each boy is asked to sign-up “sponsors,” who pledge a certain amount of money per lap (i.e., 20 cents, 50 cents) for each lap that a participant completes. Sponsors may also pledge or give a “flat donation” (i.e., $5, $10, $25) to a boy regardless of the number of laps he completes. The Jog-A-Thon must raise $75,000 in 2015, which means each boy should have a minimum of $800 as his Jog-a-Thon goal. Realizing the importance of the Jog-A-Thon, we’ve developed an assortment of marvelous prizes for participants, which are described in the 2015 Jog-a-thon Prize Booklet. The Chorus has also developed a wide array of activities and resources that, if used, will virtually guarantee that your son will achieve a goal of $800. The Jog-A-Thon requires the overall commitment and hard work of each boy and his family. Thus, we respectfully ask that each AABC family read and complete a JOG-A-THON FAMILY COMMITMENT FORM (click to download) that lists the many ways we provide to help you in your quest for sponsors. Please read, discuss and complete the form with your son. Then, please return the form to us. Together, let’s make Jog-A-Thon 2015 a magical success – one for the AABC history books! Sincerely, Tony Manrique, Executive Director
